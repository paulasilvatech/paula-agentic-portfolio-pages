05 Part 05

Integration playbook

How to wire the four layers together. 90-day roadmap, failure modes taxonomy, and a live self-assessment.

Diagnostic question
How do the layers compose, and where does the stack fail first?
14 min read 6 sections

The premise

Four layers are not four projects. They are one system. The most common failure mode in agentic platform rollouts is treating each layer as a separate initiative with a separate budget and a separate timeline. The dependencies are real: you cannot do Context (03) well without Platform (02). You cannot do Intent (04) at all without Context (03).

This chapter is the integration layer. The 90-day roadmap, the assessment that tells you where to start, and the failure modes that show up when teams skip a step.

Your maturity is the minimum of four, not the best of one

The most common self-assessment mistake is reporting the best layer as the team’s maturity. If your infrastructure is world-class but your intent layer is a Slack thread, your stack maturity is the intent layer. Agents fail at the weakest link, not the strongest.

Insight
Invest in the weakest layer first

The radar shape tells you where to invest. A lopsided radar is more dangerous than a small balanced one. Four stage-02s beats one stage-05 and three stage-01s, every time.

The 90-day roadmap

The roadmap is calibrated for a team at stage 01-02 across most layers. If you are further along, compress the timeline. If you are not yet at stage 01 on infrastructure, fix that before reading the rest.

The integration anti-patterns

Three anti-patterns cause more pain than all the individual layer mistakes combined:

  1. The platform-last anti-pattern. Team ships three agents on three different architectures, then tries to retrofit a platform. The retrofit costs more than all three agents did originally. Rule: platform begins with the second agent, not the fifth.
  2. The constitution-committee anti-pattern. Governance is a monthly architecture board instead of a file. Every decision takes a month. Agents ship anyway, outside the process. The process loses legitimacy. Rule: governance is code or it does not scale.
  3. The measurement-later anti-pattern. Team ships agents without trajectory logs, planning to “add observability in Q2.” Q2 arrives; adding logs requires rewriting every agent. Rule: instrument day one or do not ship.

Monday morning

  1. Run the self-assessment as a group. Not just leadership. Include the engineer who actually ships agents, not only the directors who pay for them. The disagreements are informative.
  2. Draft the 90-day roadmap. One page. Name owners for each artifact. Assume no new budget.
  3. Pick the weakest layer and commit. Not “we will focus on three.” One layer. Ninety days.
  4. Commit to the weekly review. Fifteen minutes, same day, same time. Intent debt, failing skills, constitution conflicts. Miss three and the program is dead.
  5. Read chapter 01 if you skipped it. Most teams that fail at this layer underinvested in layer 01. The stack is serial. The foundation matters most.
